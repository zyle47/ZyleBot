Approval Gate

Nothing that touches your files or your shell runs without you saying yes, first.

The gate, exactly

When the agent loop wants to call a CONFIRM_REQUIRED tool — write_file, append_file, make_directory, run_command — the turn pauses and a card appears showing the exact tool name and the exact arguments it intends to run. Nothing is inferred or summarized; what you see is what would execute.

Approve or deny

Approve resumes the loop with that one call executed. Deny resumes it without running anything, and the model continues from there knowing it was refused. Either way, the decision is yours alone — read-only tools never reach this gate at all.

Survives a reload

Close the tab mid-approval and reopen it — the pending confirmation re-renders exactly as you left it. The loop is genuinely paused, not just visually paused, so a refresh can't be used to skip the gate.

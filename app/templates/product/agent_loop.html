PRODUCT // 01

Agent Loop

One request in, a chain of reasoning and tool calls out — streamed to your screen as it happens, not after the fact.

Multi-step ReAct, not a single round-trip

ZyleBot doesn't just answer — it reasons, calls a tool, reads the result, and decides whether to call another one, up to AGENT_MAX_STEPS steps per turn. A question like "weather in Belgrade" can trigger a search, a fetch, and a synthesis step before you see a final answer. Identical repeat calls are skipped, and if the step cap is hit the model is forced to answer from what it already gathered — you never get stuck in a silent loop.

Reasoning streamed live, over SSE

The model's reasoning_content and its content are two separate channels, streamed token-by-token over server-sent events as they're generated. Reasoning renders dimmed and collapsible so you can watch the model think without it crowding the actual answer.

Pauses for you, not around you

When a step needs a risky tool, the loop doesn't guess — it pauses mid-turn and waits for your Approve or Deny. Nothing runs until you say so. See Approval Gate for how that gate works.

Voice and vision, same loop

Speak instead of type — audio is transcribed locally by faster-whisper running on CPU, so it never competes with the model for VRAM. Paste an image and the loop switches to vision mode for that turn. Every conversation, including its images, is persisted to SQLite so history survives a restart.
